Approach

My program is built around hunter under saddle and western pleasure — the flat classes, where self-carriage, willingness, and a level, consistent frame win over anything flashy.

Those qualities are easiest to build into a horse before bad habits exist, which is why young horse starting is such a core part of what I do. A colt started with the rail in mind from his first ride carries that foundation into every class after.

Every horse — young prospect or horse already showing — gets an individual plan based on its age, mind, and pedigree, not a one-size program run on a clock.

Young Horse Specialty

Started right, from the day they're weaned

Every young prospect moves through the same sequence toward hunter under saddle or western pleasure, starting at weaning — the pace adjusts to the horse, the order never does.

Weanling

Groundwork & Foundation

Leading, yielding, tying, and sacking out. No rider yet — just trust and mechanics, built from the very start.

Yearling

Long-Lining & Handling

Building on the foundation with long-lining and continued handling, so nothing about tack or pressure is a surprise later.

Two-Year-Old

First Rides & Basics

Mounting, steering, stopping, and building confidence at the walk, jog, and lope.

Show Ready

Cadence & Frame

Self-carriage and headset specific to hunter under saddle or western pleasure, then pattern work and ring exposure.
